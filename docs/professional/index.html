Wildfire ¬∑ Geospatial index

Wildfire Resilience Index

An index composed of high resolution (90m) spatiotemporal maps assessing wildfire resilience across western U.S. and Canada. We compiled over 100 unique geospatial datasets to assess socio-ecological wildfire resiliency. Data layers spanned domains such as infrastructure, health, vegetation, species, carbon, air, water and more.

This is a spin off project from my work on WRI. Poor air quality has negative health outcomes and is an important dimension of public health and policy, especially in relation to wildfires. The Environmental Protection Agency has developed AQI thresholds above which there are increased health risks. AQI greater than 100 aims to represent base level unhealthy air exposure for sensitive groups, while AQI greater than 300 aims to capture acute hazardous smoke events.
