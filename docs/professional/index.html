Geospatial Data Scientist

Maps, models, and stories about changing landscapes.

I work at the intersection of geospatial analysis, data visualization, and environmental science—building maps and tools that translate complex datasets into clear, actionable insights.

I'm focused on wildfire resilience, cultural heritage, and climate impacts— building static and interactive maps from hundreds of social and ecological data layers, and always looking for new ways to make spatial data intuitive and beautiful.

What I work with

Combining environmental science training with geospatial engineering to build reproducible, transparent analyses.

Geospatial & cartography — Design maps that balance scientific accuracy with clear storytelling: raster & vector processing, projections, static & interactive mapping.

Analytics & modeling — Build deterministic and index-based models that integrate social, ecological, and infrastructure data: spatial statistics, exposure & vulnerability indices, reproducible workflows.

Tools — Python, R, ArcGIS, QGIS, GeoPandas, sf, Rasterio, terra, Git & GitHub.

Selected projects

A few examples of how I use spatial data, visualization, and modeling to answer environmental questions.

Wildfire · Geospatial index

Wildfire Resilience Index

As a geospatial analyst at the National Center for Ecological Analysis and Synthesis, I worked on a nationwide index integrating nearly 100 layers across infrastructure, health, vegetation, species, air, water, and cultural heritage. Python, R, QGIS · spatial aggregation & weighting, index design & visualization.

Agriculture · Field experiment

Floral hemp nitrogen responses

Field-based analysis of floral hemp responses to nitrogen fertilization in the high desert, combining yield analytics, spatial context, and fertilization tracking. Experimental design, mixed data & spatial analysis, visualization for publications.

Climate · Cultural heritage

Cultural heritage & wildfire exposure

Nationwide assessment of how cultural heritage sites in the United States intersect with wildfire risk, combining heritage inventories with hazard and exposure modeling. Spatial joins & overlays, risk & exposure metrics, cartographic storytelling.
